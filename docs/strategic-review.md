# Strategic Review of Tsubasa AI Project

Date: 2026-05-25

## Overview
This document summarizes a structural review of the Tsubasa AI Project from three professional perspectives:

1. AI Architect Perspective
2. Business / Management Perspective
3. Programming / Product Perspective

The goal is to clarify:
- Why this project matters
- Potential risks
- Success conditions
- Recommended execution strategy

---

# 1. AI Architect Perspective

## Conclusion

The direction is strong and highly promising.

However:

> The goal should NOT be “scan 100GB of documents”.

Instead:

> The goal is to create an operational intelligence layer for the company.

This project is fundamentally not a chatbot.

It is closer to:

> A Decision-Making Operating System.

## Biggest Risk

### Scope Explosion

Trying to include:
- All documents
- All customs files
- All accounting
- All OCR
- All agents

at once will likely fail.

The biggest enemy is NOT technology.

It is:

> Data Quality

## Recommendation

Start with an extremely narrow MVP.

Example:

> ONLY Tire Price Search

If employees immediately feel:

> “This is useful.”

Then adoption starts.

---

# 2. Business / Management Perspective

## Conclusion

This project is especially suitable for a company of 10–50 employees.

## Core Value

Most SMEs suffer from:

> President Dependency

Business knowledge exists only inside the president's head.

This project effectively becomes:

> Turning the president's brain into an AI system.

## Most Valuable Idea

The strongest idea in this project:

> Understanding employee struggles.

AI becomes:

> The MRI of the organization.

## Important Warning

Employees must NEVER feel monitored.

Wrong framing:

❌ Tracking employees

Correct framing:

⭕ Reducing employee frustrations

---

# 3. Programming / Product Perspective

## Conclusion

Technically achievable.

But:

> Do NOT custom-build everything from day one.

## First Feature Recommendation

Only build:

> Tire Price Search

Nothing else.

Not:
- Finance
- Customs
- Automation
- Mascot

Just:

> “What's the latest tire price?”

## Technology Recommendation

- Mac Studio
- Dify
- OpenAI GPT
- Claude
- PostgreSQL
- pgvector
- RAG architecture

Validate business value first.

Optimize later.

---

# Structural Analysis

The project has three layers.

## Layer 1 — Knowledge Layer

Document retrieval.

Examples:
- Tire specs
- Price sheets
- SOP
- Customs documents

## Layer 2 — Decision Layer

Decision support.

Examples:
- Historical pricing
- Customer comparison
- Operational recommendations

## Layer 3 — Organization Layer

Organizational improvement.

Examples:
- Employee bottlenecks
- Recurring issues
- Automation opportunities
- Training needs

Most companies stop at Layer 1.

Tsubasa AI aims at Layer 3.

---

# Success Condition

> Start Small

Employees must quickly experience value.

---

# First 90 Days Recommendation

Only focus on:

> Tire Price Search

This becomes the project's North Star.

---

# Final Opinion

The project has strong structural logic.

It is realistic.

It matches the company size.

It can scale.

However:

> Success depends on resisting complexity in the beginning.

Start small.

Win quickly.

Expand gradually.
